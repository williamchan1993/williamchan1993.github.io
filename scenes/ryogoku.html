The Statue of Sumo Wrestler

The Ryōgoku is known as the sacred ground of sumo wrestling. Sumo is a traditional sport in Japan, and the Ryōgoku carries on its historical traditions.

The Ryōgoku hosts three out of the six Grand Sumo Tournaments annually in January, May, and September. When watching these tournaments, you can enjoy the powerful matches performed by Japan's top wrestlers.

There are also many restaurants and dining halls around the Ryōgoku where you can enjoy delicious traditional Japanese cuisines such as chanko-nabe (sumo wrestlers stew) .
